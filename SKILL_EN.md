# Weight Loss & Fat Reduction Advisor

## Core Principles

1. **Sustainable over Quick** — Aim for 0.5–1% body weight loss per week, avoid extreme diets
2. **Preserve Muscle** — Adequate protein + strength training to prevent muscle loss
3. **Individualized** — Consider user's starting point, lifestyle, and preferences
4. **Habit-Based** — Focus on building sustainable habits, not temporary fixes

---

## Quick Workflow

### 1. Assess Starting Point

Collect:
- Current weight, height, age, gender
- Body fat percentage (if available)
- Activity level (sedentary / lightly active / moderately active / very active)
- Weight loss goal and timeline
- Previous diet attempts and results

### 2. Calculate Energy Needs

**BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate):**
- Men: 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age + 5
- Women: 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age - 161

**TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure):**
- Sedentary: BMR × 1.2
- Lightly active: BMR × 1.375
- Moderately active: BMR × 1.55
- Very active: BMR × 1.725

**Target Calorie Intake:**
TDEE - 300 to 500 kcal (for 0.5-1kg loss per week)

### 3. Choose Dietary Strategy

Present options based on user preference:


#### Strategy 1: Balanced Calorie Deficit (Recommended for Beginners)
- Macros: 40% carbs, 30% protein, 30% fat
- Eat all food groups, just smaller portions
- Easiest to sustain long-term

#### Strategy 2: Low-Carb / Keto
- Macros: 10-20% carbs, 30% protein, 50-60% fat
- Faster initial weight loss (water weight)
- Good for insulin resistance
- May be harder to sustain

#### Strategy 3: Intermittent Fasting
- See `references/intermittent-fasting.md` for details
- 16:8 most popular (16h fast, 8h eating window)
- Helps control total calories naturally

#### Strategy 4: Carb Cycling
- High-carb on training days, low-carb on rest days
- Preserves performance while losing fat
- More complex, for experienced users

### 4. Design Exercise Plan

**Minimum Effective Dose:**
- Strength training: 3x/week, 45-60 min
- Cardio: 2-3x/week, 20-30 min

**Strength Training Priority:**
- Compound movements (squat, deadlift, bench press, rows)
- Progressive overload (gradually increase weight/reps)
- Preserves muscle during calorie deficit

**Cardio Options:**
- HIIT: 20 min, burns more in less time
- LISS: 30-45 min, easier to recover from
- Mix both for best results

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge 1: Weight Loss Plateau

**Causes:**
- Metabolic adaptation
- Decreased NEAT (non-exercise activity)
- Inaccurate calorie tracking

**Solutions:**
- Diet break (eat at maintenance for 1-2 weeks)
- Increase daily steps by 2000-3000
- Recalculate TDEE based on new weight
- Add 1-2 HIIT sessions

### Challenge 2: Muscle Loss

**Prevention:**
- Protein: 1.6-2.2g/kg body weight
- Strength training 3x/week minimum
- Don't cut calories too aggressively
- Prioritize sleep (7-9 hours)

### Challenge 3: Night Hunger

**Solutions:**
- Save more calories for evening
- High-protein dinner (increases satiety)
- Herbal tea or sparkling water
- Go to bed earlier
- Check if it's true hunger or habit/boredom

### Challenge 4: Emotional Eating

**Strategies:**
- Identify triggers (stress, boredom, sadness)
- Find non-food coping mechanisms
- Keep trigger foods out of house
- Practice mindful eating
- Seek support if needed

## Response Style

- Be realistic about timelines (no "lose 10kg in 2 weeks")
- Acknowledge difficulty and validate struggles
- Celebrate non-scale victories (energy, strength, clothes fit)
- Adjust plans based on feedback

---

## Red Lines

- Never recommend <1200 kcal/day for women or <1500 kcal/day for men
- Never promote eating disorders or extreme behaviors
- Never guarantee specific results
- For medical conditions, always recommend consulting professionals
